## 2. The story

**Why does this business exist?**
Gareth Hartley started as a British Gas engineer but got frustrated watching customers pay call-out fees for simple fixes he could've explained over the phone. Eight years ago he went solo to do things differently — same-day callouts in the Swindon area, upfront pricing before any work starts, and a promise to leave your home cleaner than he found it. He's built his reputation on treating every radiator leak and boiler service like it's his own house.

**What do customers most often say about them?**
"He actually explained what was wrong instead of just fixing it and charging me."
"Turned up on time, got it done same day, and didn't leave my kitchen looking like a building site."
"Honest about what needs doing now vs. what can wait — didn't try to upsell me."

**What's the unique angle?**
1. Same-day callouts in Swindon. Most plumbers book you in weeks ahead or charge premium rates for urgent work. Gareth keeps slots open for genuine emergencies and fixes them the same day at standard rates.
2. Transparent pricing before tools come out. He'll diagnose the problem, tell you exactly what it'll cost, and won't start work until you've said yes. No surprises on the invoice.

## 3. Services offered

List the core services the business provides. Group them if it helps. For each, one sentence on what it actually means.

Emergency & Urgent

Burst pipes & leaks — Same-day fix for water spraying from joints or cracked pipes, plus damage assessment.
No hot water — Diagnosis and repair of boiler or immersion heater faults (or referral to a Gas Safe engineer if it's beyond general plumbing).

Routine Maintenance

Radiator bleeding & balancing — Getting heat to all rooms evenly and clearing air locks that stop radiators warming up.
Tap repairs & replacements — Fixing drips, replacing washers, or fitting new taps without ripping out cupboards.
Toilet fixes — Leaking cisterns, broken flush mechanisms, blocked pans—diagnosed and repaired the same day.
Shower & bath servicing — Clearing blockages, fixing low pressure, replacing seals so they don't drip.

Larger Jobs

Pipe repairs & rerouting — Fixing corroded or frozen pipes, moving water lines to avoid future problems.
Bathroom suite installation — Fitting new baths, showers, or toilet suites (plumbing side only—not tiling or carpentry).

**Any specialisms or premium offerings worth highlighting?**

Gas Safe work — Gareth is Part P registered for boiler servicing and central heating jobs that touch gas. Reassures customers they're legal and insured.
Preventative site visits — For older properties, he'll do a free survey to flag upcoming issues (corroded pipes, outdated stopcock) so owners can budget ahead.

**Anything they explicitly don't do?**
Boiler installation or replacement — He diagnoses problems and fixes leaks, but new boiler fitting goes to a full Gas Safe engineer.
Plumbing design for new builds — He handles repair and maintenance, not architectural plumbing for house extensions.
Drainage (foul sewers) — Blocked drains on the mains system go to a drainage specialist; he handles internal waste pipes only.
Heating system overhauls — He maintains and repairs what's there, but doesn't redesign central heating layouts.

## 6. Visual direction

**Aesthetic direction (pick one or describe your own):**
Industrial craft — Honest, functional, skilled. Not brutalist (too cold), not retro (too nostalgic), but grounded in the reality of a tradesman's work. Think workshop competence meets approachable local business. The opposite of "sterile minimalism."

**Colour direction:**
Workshop-inspired with warmth:

Deep charcoal or slate (walls, backgrounds) — practical, serious, no-nonsense
Burnt orange or terracotta accent — tool handles, energy, warmth without being corporate
Off-white or warm grey (text, contrast) — readable, honest, not cold
Occasional brass or copper (highlights, icons) — nod to traditional plumbing fittings, craft heritage

**Reference images or sites (optional):**
Reiss (UK menswear) — restrained, quality-focused, no shouting
A Good Company (furniture) — craft + simplicity, honest about materials
Brewdog (early branding) — industrial but approachable, not sterile

**Typography direction:**
Serif or sans: Sans-serif (accessibility, modern), but not generic.
Specific fonts to explore:

Heading: Playfair Display (already in your NeoBookworm system) or Crimson Text (serif, warm, slightly old-school craft feel) — something with character
Body: DM Sans (your NeoBookworm standard) or IBM Plex Sans (slightly more personality than Inter, still highly readable)
