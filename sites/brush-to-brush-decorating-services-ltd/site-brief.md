## 2. The story

**Why does this business exist?**
Brush 2 Brush was built by two brothers-in-law who grew up in the decorating trade and wanted to run things their own way — a family business where the work speaks for itself and the price is fair. With a combined three decades of hands-on experience between them, they formalised the business in 2021 and have grown steadily through word-of-mouth and platform referrals ever since. Their philosophy is simple: you're only as good as the last job you've done.

- Clean and tidy — always leave the property spotless
- Responsive and reliable — quick to quote, turn up when they say they will
- Reasonable pricing — value for money without cutting corners
- Happy to help beyond the brief — one review mentions them removing a built-in bed as part of a decorating job

**What's the unique angle?**
Two things set them apart: (1) they're genuine specialists — wallpaper, murals, kitchen unit spraying, and artex removal are all in their wheelhouse, not just standard emulsion work; and (2) the family-run model means you're dealing with the people who actually do the work, not a faceless company that subcontracts out. Multiple reviews mention Matt by name, showing the personal relationship customers build.

## 3. Services offered

- **Interior Painting:** Full room redecorations, feature walls, ceilings, woodwork, and skirting boards — residential and commercial.
- **Exterior Painting:** Windows, doors, fascias, rendering, and full exterior repaints. Known for speed — exterior of a house completed in 4 days in one review.
- **Wallpapering:** Standard wallpaper hanging plus specialist wallpaper for feature walls and statement rooms.
- **Mural Work:** Custom murals — multiple customers have praised this as a standout offering.
- **Kitchen Unit Spraying:** Cabinet respray service as a cost-effective alternative to a full kitchen replacement.
- **Coving & Cornices:** Supply and installation of decorative coving and cornice mouldings.
- **Artex Removal:** Safe removal of textured Artex ceilings and walls for a modern smooth finish.
- **Landlord & Property Services:** Decorating for buy-to-let, HMO, and rental property turnarounds between tenancies.
- **Hard Flooring:** Fitting of hard flooring (laminate, vinyl, engineered wood).
- **Handyman Services:** General property maintenance and odd jobs — light carpentry, fixture hanging, minor repairs.

**Any specialisms or premium offerings worth highlighting?**
Mural work and kitchen unit spraying are the two premium differentiators — most painter-decorators in Bristol don't offer either. The landlord/BTL/HMO service also shows commercial awareness that distinguishes them from purely domestic operators.

**Anything they explicitly don't do?**
No evidence of plumbing, electrics, or structural work. They appear to refer out specialist trades (one review mentions a separate electrician working alongside them).

## 6. Visual direction

**Aesthetic direction:**
Warm craft meets honest-work industrial. Think clean white walls with paint-roller texture in the background, the satisfying straight edge of freshly cut-in paintwork, and the warmth of a well-decorated family home. Not slick agency; not builder-rough either. Somewhere in between — craft-proud but approachable.

**Colour direction:**
Deep navy or charcoal as the primary dark tone (professional, grounding), paired with a warm ochre or burnt orange accent (craft warmth, energy), and clean off-white backgrounds. Think of a well-decorated Bristol Victorian terrace — the colour palette of the homes they work in, not a corporate brochure.

**Reference images or sites (optional):**
Bristles of Bristol (bristlesofbristol.co.uk) is a local competitor with a clean, friendly site that captures a similar market. Coopers Decorating (coopers-decorating.co.uk) has a slightly more corporate feel. The Brush 2 Brush demo should sit between these — warmer and more personal than Coopers, slightly more polished than a basic trade site.

**Typography direction:**
A solid, slightly rounded sans-serif for headings (something with warmth — not geometric/cold). A clean readable sans for body. Nothing too formal or serif-heavy — this is a working trade, not a law firm.
