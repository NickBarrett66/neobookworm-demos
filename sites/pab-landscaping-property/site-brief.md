## 6. Visual direction
**Aesthetic direction:**  
<mark>Grounded and honest. No lifestyle photography, no mood boards. This site should feel like the work itself — solid, well-finished, properly done. Think natural materials: stone, timber, earth. Clean layout with generous whitespace, but warm-toned rather than cold and minimal. The Cotswolds context matters: this is rural England, working landscape, not urban garden design.</mark>

**Colour direction:**  
<mark>Deep forest green primary — the colour of well-kept lawns and hedgerows. Warm slate grey secondary. Cream or natural linen background — not white. Earthy, grounded, not corporate.</mark>

**Reference images or sites (optional):**  
Gloucester Garden Design Company (gloucestergardendesigncompany.co.uk) — more premium than P.A.B but good regional reference for the market. P.A.B should be positioned as more approachable and honest, less aspirational lifestyle.

**Typography direction:**  
<mark>Display: a slightly characterful serif — something with weight and confidence, not a clean sans. Suggest Playfair Display or Cormorant Garamond for headings. Body: a legible humanist sans — Lato or Source Sans Pro. The contrast should feel craftsman-made, not tech-startup.</mark>
