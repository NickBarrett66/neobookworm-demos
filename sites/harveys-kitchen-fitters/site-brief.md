## 6. Visual direction
**Aesthetic direction:**
**Warm craft portfolio** — this is not a Harvey Jones showroom and it shouldn't look like one. The site should feel like the portfolio of a trusted local tradesman who takes real pride in real work. Think: actual kitchen photography (not stock), natural materials (timber, stone worktops), warm tones. It should feel rooted in Bath's Georgian and Victorian housing stock — solid, unhurried, built to last. Deliberately not: clinical white-on-white, aspirational lifestyle photography, or anything that feels like a Wren Kitchens microsite.

**Colour direction:**
 Warm off-whites and cream, paired with a grounded deep tone — forest green, dark slate, or charcoal-brown. Earthy and honest. Bath stone as an ambient reference. Avoids the cold blues and greys that dominate national chains.
