## 3. Services offered

- **Boiler installation and replacement:** Full removal of old system and installation of a new gas boiler — combi, system, or conventional — with commissioning and handover.
- **Central heating installation:** New or upgraded central heating systems including radiators, pipework, thermostats, and smart controls.
- **Boiler servicing:** Annual service to keep the boiler running efficiently, extend its lifespan, and maintain manufacturer warranty.
- **Boiler repair and breakdown:** Fault diagnosis and repair for boilers that won't fire, aren't heating properly, or have lost pressure.
- **Gas safety certificates:** Annual Gas Safe inspection and CP12 certificate for landlords — required by law for rental properties.
- **Plumbing repairs:** Leaking pipes, dripping taps, running toilets, pressure issues — the full range of domestic plumbing faults.
- **Bathroom plumbing:** Fitting new shower valves, bath taps, basins, toilets — typically as part of a bathroom installation or one-off replacement.
- **Smart thermostat installation:** Fitting and commissioning Nest, Hive, or similar smart heating controls.
- **Power flushing:** Cleaning out sludge and debris from older central heating systems to restore heat output and efficiency.

**Any specialisms or premium offerings worth highlighting?**
Yes — two specific ones confirmed from his own words:

1. **Alpha boiler installation with up to 13-year warranty.** He specifically installs Alpha boilers, citing their stainless steel heat exchanger and competitive pricing. 13 years is an exceptional warranty length and a genuine sales differentiator — most boiler installers can only offer 5–10 years. This should get a dedicated callout block on the site, not just a bullet in the services list.
2. **Gas safety certificates for landlords (CP12).** Reliable, recurring, contractual revenue — worth a dedicated callout if Lee does this work. Standard for a Gas Safe engineer covering Wootton Bassett and the Swindon rental market.

> ⚠️ **ASSUMPTION on landlord certs:** Not explicitly mentioned in the interview but near-universal for Gas Safe engineers. Confirm before publishing.

**Anything they explicitly don't do?**
Oil boilers, OFTEC, commercial large-scale systems.

> ⚠️ **ASSUMPTION:** Gas Safe registration covers gas appliances only, so oil is out unless he also holds OFTEC. The "gasman" identity further reinforces gas-only focus. Verify before publishing.

## 6. Visual direction

**Aesthetic direction:**
Warm craft with industrial undertones. This isn't a slick corporate plumbing chain and shouldn't look like one. Think: a tradesman who takes pride in the work, takes pride in the van being clean, takes pride in the site not looking like it was built in 2009. Approachable and tactile — slightly warm, not sterile.

**Colour direction:**
Deep slate navy as the primary — authoritative, trustworthy, reads "qualified professional" rather than "budget callout." Offset with a warm off-white or parchment rather than pure white — removes the clinical edge. A single warm accent colour (burnt amber or copper) to nod to pipework and trade craftsmanship without being too literal.

This is *not* the same as the red-and-navy franchise aesthetic (British Gas, Boiler Plan, etc.) — avoid that palette entirely.

**Reference images or sites (optional):**
None confirmed. For vibe reference: look at how independent joinery or cabinet-making businesses present themselves in the UK — that "craftsman who takes the work seriously" energy translated to a heating engineer.

**Typography direction:**
A restrained sans-serif for body text — something with slightly more personality than system defaults, but not a display face. Pair with a sturdy, slightly condensed heading face that feels grounded rather than elegant. We want the typography to feel like it was chosen by someone who pays attention, not by someone who grabbed the first Google Font. Avoid anything that reads as tech-startup or fashion-adjacent.

## 9. Anything else

**The backstory is the site's strongest asset.** The Alpha interview gives us something most competitor sites will never have: a real, specific, human story. He didn't grow up wanting to be a gas engineer — he fell into it through his dad. He spent a decade at one company. When it folded, he didn't look for another employer; he built his own thing. That narrative arc (trade roots → long career → redundancy → going it alone → 100% recommendation rate) is the About page, almost word for word. It's the difference between a credible demo and a generic one.

**The "gasman" identity is a genuine asset.** His email handle (leemorgangasman) and Facebook identity have already done the brand work. The site can lean into this: *"When people need a gas engineer in Wootton Bassett, they call the gasman."* Not as a headline — woven into copy as a quiet thread.

**The 13-year warranty deserves prominent placement.** Most boiler installers offer 5–7 years. 13 years is exceptional and specific. It should be in the hero section or immediately below it, not buried in the services list. The line almost writes itself: *"Alpha boilers. Up to 13 years covered. Peace of mind built in."*

**The flue gas analyser detail is worth a subtle nod.** When asked what tool he can't live without, Lee cited his flue gas analyser — not a pipe cutter or spanners, but an instrument of precision and compliance. For an engineer's About or Services page, a single line like *"Every installation is commissioned with a flue gas analyser — not because we have to, but because it matters"* signals the kind of thoroughness that separates him from the cowboys.

**Domain recommendation:** `leemorgangasman.co.uk` is available. If either is available, mention it in the email. Offering to include the domain as part of the package removes a friction point for someone who's never bought a domain.

**Competitor framing:** The main established player in RWB — CentraHeat — has a full Which? Trusted Trader profile and a polished website. The pitch to Lee isn't "beat CentraHeat." It's: *you've spent 18 years building expertise and 4 years building a reputation, and right now none of it shows up when someone searches for a gas engineer in Wootton Bassett.* The work is done. The site just needs to exist.
