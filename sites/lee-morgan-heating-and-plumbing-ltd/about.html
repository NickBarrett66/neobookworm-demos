The story behind the name

I trained as a printer. Weekends I was on the tools with my dad. He was a plumber and the trade got into my blood.

I qualified in gas, worked in the industry for 18 years, then the company I’d been with for 10 years closed down and I was made redundant. In March 2021 I went out on my own. Same standards. Just my name on the van.

Why “gasman”?

People started calling me that years ago. It stuck. If you need a gas engineer locally, you don’t want a call centre — you want the person who turns up and sorts it.

How I work

I treat every job the same way my dad taught me: do it properly, leave it tidy, and explain what I’ve done.

  • Turn up when I say I will
  • Explain the fault in plain English
  • Commission and test, not guess
  • Leave your place clean

Every day is a school day. I still learn on the job — that’s how you stay sharp.
