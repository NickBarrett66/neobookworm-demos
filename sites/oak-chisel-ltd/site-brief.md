## 2. The story

**Why does this business exist?**  
Bobbie has been working with wood for 14 years — fitting kitchens, making joinery, doing the detailed hand-finished work that most firms don't bother with. Oak & Chisel Ltd was incorporated in early 2023, formalising what was already a going concern. The name signals a deliberate craft identity: not a generic "property services" trading name, but a statement about materials and method. 

**What do customers most often say about them?**  
No public reviews found across Google, Checkatrade, TrustATrader, or MyBuilder. Entirely word-of-mouth and direct-referral at this stage. Facebook page has 178 likes; Instagram has 117 posts and 90 followers under @oakandchiselcarpentry. The Instagram bio reads: *"Witney based carpenter and kitchen fitter with 14yrs of experience in making and fitting of joinery. Please drop a message for a quote."*

**What's the unique angle?**  
14 years of experience in a sole-operator format means Bobbie does the work — not a team of subcontractors. Kitchen fitting and carpentry together under one person means a seamless job: the person fitting your kitchen is the same person who made or selected the joinery components. Custom domain email (bobbie@oakchisel.co.uk) suggests a level of professional infrastructure unusual for a solo operator with no website.

## 6. Visual direction

**Aesthetic direction:**  
Honest-materials workshop aesthetic — think raw oak grain, clean pencil lines, the satisfying geometry of well-fitted joinery. Not rustic/country; not sleek Scandinavian minimalism. Something in between: the aesthetic of a workshop that takes itself seriously. Natural light, close-up timber details, visible craft. 

**Colour direction:**  
Warm mid-tone: oak/amber grain tones as the dominant warmth, offset with off-white or warm light grey and a single dark anchor (near-black or deep warm charcoal). Avoid blue. Avoid anything that reads as corporate or kitchen-showroom. 

**Reference images or sites (optional):**  
unknown

**Typography direction:**  
A sturdy serif with visible character for headings (something with ink-trap personality — Freight Display, Canela, or a geometric slab like Playfair Display with weight) paired with a legible humanist sans for body copy (Neue Haas Grotesk, Source Sans 3, or Lato). No default system fonts. No Inter.
