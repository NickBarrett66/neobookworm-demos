## 6. Visual direction
**Aesthetic direction:**  
<mark>Working-class craft pride — clean, dark, and confident. Not a corporate navy-and-white template. Think deep charcoal backgrounds with warm amber or orange accents (flame/heat reference), bold chunky type, and the kind of layout that says "this lad knows what he's doing." No stock photography smiles. No gradients. The visual equivalent of a freshly lagged pipe — functional, tidy, no fuss.</mark>

**Colour direction:**  
<mark>Charcoal / near-black hero (#1a1a1a or similar), with warm amber or burnt orange as the primary accent. White body text. No pastel anything. Trust strip in a slightly lighter dark tone. The palette should evoke heat and competence, not a bank brochure.</mark>

**Reference images or sites (optional):**  
Competitors to contrast *against*: Mr Therm (established but dated, corporate feel), TVM Plumbing (family-values template), Scunthorpe Boilers (functional but generic). DWW's site should feel more individual — closer in confidence to a skilled tradesperson's personal brand than a multi-van operation's marketing site.

**Typography direction:**  
<mark>Display: something with weight and edge — a condensed sans or a geometric bold (e.g. Barlow Condensed, Space Grotesk, or similar). No Inter. No Roboto. Body: clean, legible sans at readable size. The headline type should feel like it means business; the body should stay out of the way.</mark>
