## 2. The story

**Why does this business exist?**
Daniel spent five years working for a larger regional firm where the vans were branded but the workmanship was inconsistent and customers were handled like numbers. He set up Swift Electrical because he believed a small team could do better work *and* better communication — and that being on time, sending a written quote before touching anything, and cleaning up properly after every job were simply the minimum standard, not a selling point. The Cotswolds market rewarded that: word of mouth kept the diary full within 18 months, and he took on a second electrician two years later.

**What do customers most often say about them?**
"Daniel was here exactly when he said he'd be."
"We got a written quote the same evening, which I wasn't expecting."
"The guys left it cleaner than they found it — I'm not joking."
"Recommended by our neighbour, and now I've recommended them to three people."

**What's the unique angle?**
Two specifics: (1) **Same-day written quotes** — every visit results in a quote emailed that evening, not a verbal promise followed by silence. (2) **Cotswold property experience** — solid knowledge of older stone properties, their quirks (thick walls, old wiring buried in odd places, no cavity insulation to tuck cables into), which means fewer surprises mid-job.

## 3. Services offered

- **Consumer unit (fuse board) upgrades** — replacing old fuse boards with modern RCD-protected units; includes inspection and certification.
- **Rewires (full and partial)** — whole-house rewires and targeted partial rewires for extensions or older properties with problem circuits.
- **EV charger installation** — home and small business charge point installation; OZEV-registered, handles grant paperwork.
- **Additional sockets and circuits** — new double sockets, USB outlets, dedicated circuits for home offices, kitchen appliances, outbuildings.
- **Lighting design and installation** — downlights, LED retrofits, garden and driveway lighting, smart lighting wiring.
- **Fault finding and repairs** — tripping circuits, dead sockets, flickering lights, partial power loss; diagnosed and fixed, not patched.
- **Periodic inspection reports (EICR)** — landlord certificates, pre-purchase inspections, insurance renewals; report delivered within 48 hours.
- **Outdoor and garden electrics** — weatherproof sockets, garden buildings, outbuildings, garages; suitable for Cotswold stone outbuildings.
- **Smart home wiring** — pre-wiring for smart switches, multi-room audio, CCTV rough-in, structured cabling for home networks.

**Any specialisms or premium offerings worth highlighting?**
EV charger installation is growing fast in the area — Daniel is OZEV-registered and has installed 60+ home charge points in the last two years. Cotswold older property expertise is worth prominent mention.

**Anything they explicitly don't do?**
No commercial projects over 10 employees / light industrial. No emergency callouts after 7pm (they have families; the website is honest about this).

## 6. Visual direction

**Aesthetic direction:**
Clean & Professional — but with real warmth. Not clinical white-box corporate, but light, airy, and organised in the way a tidy office with a view over a Cotswold high street feels. Think: good stationery, natural light, calm confidence.

**Colour direction:**
Crisp white and light warm-grey as the primary canvas. A deep, quiet navy as the structural anchor (headers, nav, key text) — not the NeoBookworm navy, lighter and slightly cooler. A single warm accent: a muted sage green that nods to the Gloucestershire countryside without going rustic. Small use of amber/gold only for certification badge moments. The overall feel is: "architectural practice reception" not "plumber's van."

**Reference images or sites (optional):**
The vibe of a well-designed RIBA-listed architectural practice brochure; or a high-end local solicitors' website. Clean grid, generous white space, understated but not empty.

**Typography direction:**
A geometric sans for headings — structured and modern without being cold (something like Plus Jakarta Sans or DM Sans at bold weights). A slightly warmer, more readable sans for body copy. No serifs — serifs would push it towards heritage/craft (Hartley Plumbing's territory). The type should feel organised, not designed.

## 9. Anything else

The most important thing this site has to communicate is **reliability through process** — not just "we do good work" but "here is specifically what happens when you contact us, and we follow that process every single time." The written quote by end-of-day, the Part P certificate issued on completion, the 48-hour EICR turnaround — these are all process promises, not vague claims, and they should be presented as concrete steps rather than marketing bullets.

The site should feel like the business: calm, clean, and quietly confident that the work speaks for itself.

Avoid anything that positions Swift Electrical as a budget option — the Cirencester/Cotswolds market is comfortable; customers are choosing on trust and communication, not price. The pricing page (or pricing section) should acknowledge that they're not the cheapest and briefly explain why that's actually good news for the customer.

One phrase Daniel uses on the phone that captures the voice exactly: *"We'll get that sorted properly."* Not "we'll sort that out" — *properly*. It's the adverb that matters.
