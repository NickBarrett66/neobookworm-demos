## 6. Visual direction
**Aesthetic direction:**
<mark>Solid and unflashy — the visual equivalent of a well-finished room. Think clean photography of completed rooms (polished concrete, tiled bathrooms, fitted kitchens), warm neutrals, and a layout that suggests confidence without luxury. This is not a premium design-build studio; it's a business that gets things done properly for real families. The site should feel like the work: neat, competent, no corners cut.</mark>

**Colour direction:**
<mark>Warm off-whites, slate grey, and a single mid-tone accent (burnt orange or deep teal) — grounded and residential, not corporate. Avoids clinical white and avoids the builder-template red/yellow palette entirely.</mark>
