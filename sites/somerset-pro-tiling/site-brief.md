## 6. Visual direction
**Aesthetic direction:**  
Quiet craft. Think the visual language of a good tile showroom — clean white space, warm stone tones, close-up texture shots. Not a builder's merchant site. Not a luxury bathroom brand. The space in between: honest materials, honest work, beautifully presented. Somerset rural context: understated, a little warm, grounded.

**Colour direction:**  
Warm off-white or pale limestone as the base. A single anchoring tone — deep charcoal or slate grey for headings and nav. Optional warm terracotta or warm stone accent, used sparingly. Nothing clinical, nothing loud. Should feel like the tiles he works with.

**Reference images or sites (optional):**  
The Tile Association website (tiles.org.uk) has the right professional register. Trojan Ceramics (competitor, Bruton) has a basic site — aim above it. CD Tiling (Yeovil) is template-heavy — avoid that feel.

**Typography direction:**  
Display: a serif with some warmth — something like Playfair Display or a transitional serif (not slab). Gives craft and permanence without being fussy. Body: a clean humanist sans — Nunito, Lato, or similar. Never Inter, never Roboto.
