## 6. Visual direction

**Aesthetic direction:**
<mark>Craft-anchored interiors. Not minimalist for its own sake, not the glossy developer-spec look. Think: the satisfying detail shot — a cabinet hinge perfectly flush, a worktop edge cleanly mitred. The aesthetic should signal that these are people who care about the finish, not just the footprint. Dark backgrounds work well (charcoal, slate) to make the timber and cabinetry photographs pop. The hero should feel like a kitchen you'd actually want to cook in, not a showroom display nobody lives in.</mark>

**Colour direction:**
<mark>Deep charcoal or slate as the dominant tone — premium without being cold. Warm off-white or cream as the relief. Accent in a muted brass or warm oak tone to echo the timber and hardware theme. Avoid pure black (too stark) and any shade of beige that reads as dated.</mark>

**Reference images or sites (optional):** The Leicht Ceres Platinum kitchen shown in their September 2024 Facebook post is a strong visual reference — contemporary handles, muted palette, quality materials. That's the register to hit.

**Typography direction:**
<mark>Display: a geometric sans with character — something like DM Sans, Manrope, or Syne. Should feel modern but not clinical. Body: a legible humanist sans — Nunito Sans or IBM Plex Sans. The combination should read as "professional people who take the work seriously" without veering into either tradesperson-template territory or high-end interior design studio territory. No Inter. No Roboto.</mark>
