The business

About A P Brickwork

A Shinfield-based craftsman — fifteen years of repeat work on the back of solid, no-drama groundwork.

Since 2011

A P Brickwork — incorporated as A & P Brickwork Ltd (Companies House 07731537) — has been laying driveways, patios, and block paving across Reading and Berkshire since August 2011. That's fifteen years of jobs completed, calls returned, and sub-bases that haven't shifted.

The business is owner-operated and based in Shinfield. Most work comes from repeat customers and referrals in the RG postcode area — Reading, Earley, Caversham, Wokingham, and Bracknell. There's no sales team and no subcontracting — what you discuss is what turns up.

The specialism

The permeable-driveways.co.uk domain is the business's contact email address — and it reflects where the technical focus sits. Permeable and SuDS-aware surfacing is built into how we spec every job, not sold as a premium option. Regulation increasingly expects it, and a properly drained driveway simply lasts longer.

Traditional block paving, patios, garden walls, and groundworks remain core work. The specialism in drainage isn't a marketing angle — it's fifteen years of knowing where most driveways fail, and starting there.

"A tradesman who's been doing this long enough to not need to shout about it — measured, precise, and quietly proud of the work rather than the pitch."

How we work

Four words that describe the approach — chosen from the brief, not invented for the website.

  • Understated
  • Technically competent
  • Local
  • Dependable
No drama on site

Jobs are scoped, priced, and delivered without creep or surprises. If something changes, you're told before work continues — not after.

The sub-base matters

We don't reduce excavation depth to save time. Fifteen years of installs tells you exactly where driveways fail — and it's rarely the surface material.

Local, with local knowledge

We know the soil and drainage conditions across the RG area. That matters when specifying sub-base depth, falls, and drainage runs.
