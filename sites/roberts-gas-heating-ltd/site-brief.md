## 6. Visual direction
**Aesthetic direction:**
<mark>Solid and no-nonsense — think blueprint-dark with warm accent: deep navy or charcoal base, a single warm amber or burnt orange used sparingly for calls to action. The visual language of a gas engineer who takes their work seriously. Clean layout, minimal decoration. Not corporate-polished, not rough-around-the-edges — confident and credible.</mark>

**Colour direction:**
<mark>Deep navy (#1a2b3c range) or near-black charcoal as base; warm amber/burnt orange (#d97706 range) as accent. White text. No gradients, no stock-photo blues. Think: a Gas Safe card colour palette — functional, trustworthy, warm.</mark>

**Reference images or sites (optional):**
Competitors to deliberately not resemble: Dyers (Sherborne) — polished and corporate; Carbin Heating — large team, highly branded; Bamfords — Gold Worcester installer, premium positioning. Roberts should feel like the smart alternative to the big names — personal, accountable, local.

**Typography direction:**
<mark>Display: something with quiet authority — Raleway SemiBold or DM Sans Medium (strong but not shouty). Body: Lato or Source Sans 3 — readable, unfussy. No decorative fonts, no script. The headline shouldn't look like it's trying. It should look like it already knows.</mark>
