## 3. Services offered

- **Dormer loft conversions:** The most popular type — a box-shaped extension to the roofline that maximises floor space and headroom. Suits most semi-detached and terraced properties across Bath and Wiltshire.
- **Velux / rooflight conversions:** The lower-cost option where the existing roofline stays intact and Velux windows are fitted. Ideal where headroom is already good or planning restrictions apply.
- **Hip-to-gable conversions:** Extends a hipped roof to the gable end — maximum space gain, best return on investment. Common on detached and semi-detached properties in the area.
- **Loft bedrooms:** Full habitable room with en-suite, wardrobe, and staircase — the most common request from growing families.
- **Home office loft conversions:** Dedicated workspace away from the main living areas, with power, lighting, and proper insulation for year-round use.
- **House extensions:** Single and double-storey rear and side extensions. Full build from foundations up.

## 9. Anything else

The competitors in this market all lead with "10-year guarantee" and "15 years' experience." Frayne Lofts can't compete on years (7 years trading) but can compete on something none of them say: **owner-led, every job**. That should be threaded through the site consistently.

The Bath market specifically: many of the homes in the area are Georgian or Victorian, which means planning considerations, conservation areas, and the aesthetic expectation that a loft conversion should respect the existing architecture rather than just adding a box to the roof. If Oliver has experience with this type of property, it's worth foregrounding.

The Gmail address (fraynelofts@gmail.com) is slightly informal for a Ltd company — the site copy should subtly compensate by being professional and considered, not over-corporate. The work speaks louder than the email domain.

One more note: Bath loft conversion average costs are ~£40,000 (Checkatrade data). This is a high-value trade. The site should feel like it belongs at that price point, not like a flyer pushed through a letterbox.
